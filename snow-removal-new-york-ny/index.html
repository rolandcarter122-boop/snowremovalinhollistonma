Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use your winter storm management service even after snow has already covered my property?

Yes, you can. Even if snow or ice has already formed on your property, our team can step in. We'll clear the buildup and treat the area to help prevent further snow and ice from sticking during future storms.

What does your snow and ice treatment involve after clearing my property?

After we remove the snow and ice, we apply eco-friendly de-icing agents and anti-icing treatments that help stop new buildup from forming. This keeps your walkways, driveways, and entrances safer and easier to maintain throughout the winter.

Is it possible for you to plow only my driveway and not the whole property?

We certainly can! We'll concentrate on shoveling your driveway and ignore the rest of your property if that's all you need done. Because our service is adaptable, you can tailor it to your specific needs, whether it clearing the entire property or only the driveway or walkways.

When working in the snow, what precautions does your crew take to keep from freezing?

Our group arrives ready for the cold. To be warm and dry, we prepare with thermal layers, waterproof boots and gloves, and insulated apparel. In order to prevent extended exposure, we also operate in shifts, take frequent warm-ups, and have heated equipment ready for use in the event of severe weather.

Are your services flexible?

Absolutely! Our services are flexible, you get to choose what you want us to handle.Whether it's just plowing your property or a full service including plowing and hauling.

New York, NY Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

New York, often called New York City or NYC, is the most populous city in the United States. With a 2020 population of 8,804,190 distributed over 300.46 square miles (778.2 km2), the city is the most densely populated major city in the United States. NYC is more than twice as populous as Los Angeles, the nation's second-largest city. New York City is at the southern tip of New York State and is situated on one of the world's largest natural harbors. The city comprises five boroughs, each of which is coextensive with a respective county. The five boroughs, which were created in 1898 when local governments were consolidated into a single municipality, are: Brooklyn (Kings County), Queens (Queens County), Manhattan (New York County), the Bronx (Bronx County), and Staten Island (Richmond County). New York City is a global city and a cultural, financial, high-tech, entertainment, glamour, and media center with a significant influence on commerce, health care and scientific output in life sciences, research, technology, education, politics, tourism, dining, art, fashion, and sports. Home to the headquarters of the United Nations, New York is an important center for international diplomacy, and it is sometimes described as the world's most important city and the capital of the world.
